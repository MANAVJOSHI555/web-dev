Milkha Singh

"The Flying Sikh"

Milkha Singh, also known as "The Flying Sikh," was one of India's greatest athletes. He made India proud with his exceptional achievements in track and field and inspired generations with his determination and spirit.

About Milkha Singh

Milkha Singh was born on 20 November 1929, in Govindpura, a village in Punjab, British India. Despite facing numerous challenges in life, including the Partition of India, he rose to become one of the best sprinters in the world. Milkha Singh is best remembered for his performance at the 1960 Rome Olympics, where he narrowly missed a bronze medal in the 400 meters final.

Achievements

  • Gold medal in the 1958 Asian Games (200m and 400m)
  • Gold medal in the 1958 Commonwealth Games (400m)
  • Gold medal in the 1962 Asian Games (400m and 4x400m relay)
  • Set a national record that stood for nearly 40 years
  • Padma Shri Award in 1959

Life Timeline

  • 1929: Born in Govindpura, Punjab
  • 1956: Represented India in the Melbourne Olympics
  • 1958: Won two gold medals in the Asian Games and one in the Commonwealth Games
  • 1960: Finished 4th in the Rome Olympics 400m final
  • 1998: His autobiography "The Race of My Life" was published
  • 2021: Passed away on June 18 at the age of 91
